# HR-Attrition-Analytics-Dashboard
HR Attrition Analytics Dashboard

Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition and workforce trends using HR data. The dashboard was developed to help HR teams understand employee turnover, workforce demographics, salary distribution, job satisfaction, and key retention factors.

The project combines Python, SQL, and Power BI to transform raw employee data into actionable business insights.

Tools & Technologies

- Python (Data Cleaning & Preprocessing)
- SQL (Data Analysis)
- Power BI (Interactive Dashboard Development)

Dashboard Pages

Page 1: HR Attrition Analytics

Key Metrics:

- Total Employees: 1470
- Employees Left: 237
- Attrition Rate: 16.12%
- Average Salary: 6.50K

Analysis Includes:

- Business Travel vs Attrition
- Work-Life Balance vs Attrition
- Overtime vs Attrition
- Attrition by Job Role
- Attrition by Department
- Attrition by Gender

Key Findings:

- Employees working overtime show higher attrition.
- Frequent business travel is associated with increased turnover.
- Better work-life balance contributes to employee retention.
- Research & Development has the largest workforce.

Page 2: Employee Insights Dashboard

Key Metrics:

- Average Tenure: 7.01 Years
- Average Salary: 6.50K
- Average Experience: 11.28 Years

Analysis Includes:

- Average Salary by Department
- Job Satisfaction Distribution
- Employee Tenure Distribution
- Average Salary by Job Role
- Education Field Distribution

Key Findings:

- Managers receive the highest average salary.
- Research & Development shows strong salary levels.
- Average employee tenure is approximately 7 years.
- Life Sciences is the most common education field.
